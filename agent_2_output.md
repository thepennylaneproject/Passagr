Why This Is a Filter, Not a Quiz

Each question drops one or more Path 3 destinations before any ranking occurs; no item is included
merely for personalization or engagement. The intake deliberately targets the administrative
realities captured in the research—presence requirements, lineage documentation, recognized
persecution grounds, evidentiary needs, procedural compliance, and reproductive freedom risk
profiles. Nothing here feeds scoring, aspiration, or lifestyle preference; once an answer fails,
the associated routes are removed, ensuring the downstream system only ranks viable, safety-
aligned options. The logic stays tethered to documented demands (first-step filings, EU/IRCC/COMAR
rules, reproductive rights statuses) so the intake enforces practical “hard” exclusions rather
than exploratory surveying.

Known Ambiguities (all labeled [Inference])

- Physical presence requirements may be interpreted differently at borders versus inland offices;
  we treat the research’s emphasis on “[must be physically present]” as non-negotiable, so we
  filter accordingly rather than assume leniency. [Inference]
- Documentation standards vary by country and interviewer discretion; our filter assumes the lack
  of credible evidence precludes filing, aligning with the research’s focus on official reports,
  even though ad hoc discretion can loosen enforcement. [Inference]
- Some jurisdictions (e.g., Mexico, Costa Rica) still refine how they handle reproductive freedom
  claims; we flag reproductive-risk questions as eliminating highly restricted countries,
  acknowledging that on-the-ground enforcement could vary but erring on safety-first exclusion.
  [Inference]
- Follow-up procedural schedules (medical checks/biometrics) may be postponed in emergencies, yet
  we filter out users lacking reliable support because missing those steps often leads to
  automatic denial per the documented agency workflows. [Inference]
