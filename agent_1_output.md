Safety Baseline
Source fields: “A. Global Country Safety & Protection Data” (LGBTQ+/abortion/hate-crime scores)
plus “Key Findings & Recommendations” for the badge leaders and “Data Quality & Limitations” for
conditional statuses. Passagr must exclude any extrapolation to countries or legal changes not
enumerated in the research (no new jurisdictions or post-2026 laws). Ethical drift occurs if
safety indicators are diluted to accelerate product output—e.g., dropping hate-crime or
reproductive detail for the sake of brevity or marketing.

Remote Resilience Pathways
Source fields: “B. Pathway 1” entries on income minima, timeline, and PR paths, plus “Key
Findings” notes on fastest pathways and income levels. Passagr must exclude recommending
unverifiable income figures or suggesting new visa categories; only countries in the study are
eligible. Ethical drift would be presenting these financial thresholds as evergreen guarantees
(ignoring the cited annual adjustment and backlog notes) or using them to push monetized
“opportunity” messaging.

Employment Mobility Paths
Source fields: “C. Pathway 2” job-offer requirements and processing times, with “Key Findings”
remarks on backlog delays and real-world ranges. Passagr must exclude any claim that job offers
are optional where the research says “Yes,” and must not propose speculative employment programs.
Ethical drift happens if employment-speed narratives obscure that Canada and Australia currently
face 8–18‑month backlogs, misleading users into over-optimistic timelines.

Protection Surge Protocols
Source fields: “D. Pathway 3” humanitarian entry data, “Key Findings” on EU Pact timing and
refugee commissions, and associated protection commission citations. Passagr must exclude any
pseudo-asylum shortcuts (e.g., recommending artifice routes) and must not broaden criteria beyond
the described physical-presence or ancestry rules. Ethical drift is manifested by minimizing the
need for physical presence/documentation or implying certainty for conditional legal areas (e.g.,
Italy’s march 2025 ancestry cap).

Data Integrity Constraints
Source fields: “Methodology & Sources,” “Key Findings & Recommendations,” “Data Quality &
Limitations,” and “Citations.” Passagr must exclude unreferenced facts, speculative forecasts, or
monetization justifications outside the research. Ethical drift would be treating conditional laws
(e.g., EU Pact procedures, backlog warnings, Italy ancestry reforms) as immutable “facts” or
hiding their conditionality from users.

Exclusion list

- Refuse any analysis that introduces countries or pathways absent from the supplied research
  table.
- Refuse to present unreferenced, speculative, or monetized claims (no future monetization
  assumptions).
- Refuse to turn the spine into UI/marketing features; it remains a product-primitive blueprint.

Ethical Failure Modes

- Presenting outdated processing assumptions without flagging the “current as of January 2026”
  qualifier would misuse the backlog data, leading to ethically blind promises.
- Omitting conditional language around abortion or asylum laws (which the research labels as
  “conditional,” “highly restricted,” or “reform pending”) would turn legal nuance into false
  certainty.
