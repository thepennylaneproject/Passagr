# Safety-First Output Framing: Quick Reference & Implementation Guide

## For Content & Product Teams: How to Apply This Framework

---

## The Four Required Sections (Every Result Card)

### **A: Why This Option Is Safer Than The U.S.**
**What to include:**
- Specific law or statute (name + date enacted)
- What it protects (not just topic; actual coverage)
- Source (government ministry or recognized NGO)
- How it differs from U.S. law

**What to avoid:**
- Vague claims ("very safe," "welcoming," "inclusive")
- Emotional framing ("you'll feel safe")
- Conflating law with enforcement ("safe because the law exists")

**Template trigger phrase:**
> "[COUNTRY] has [SPECIFIC PROTECTION] as of [DATE]. This differs from U.S. law because [DIFFERENCE]. Current status: [HIGH/MODERATE confidence]; [DATE updated]."

---

### **B: What Makes It Difficult**
**What to include:**
- Barriers tied to one or more intake questions
- For each barrier: the requirement, the difficulty, the timeline
- Which users face which barriers (reference intake answers)

**What to avoid:**
- Softening language ("just need," "simply provide," "fairly routine")
- Listing all possible barriers (only those relevant to user's intake answers)
- Omitting emotional/financial cost of barriers

**Template trigger phrase:**
> "The [PATHWAY] requires: 1. [REQUIREMENT] — must [ACTION]. *Difficulty:* [WHY HARD, tied to intake Q]. *Timeline:* [DATA from research]."

---

### **C: The Fastest Viable Legal Pathway**
**What to include:**
- Pathway name (official government name)
- Total timeline from start to outcome (in months/weeks)
- Current status: standard, backlog, fast-track (January 2026 data)
- Step-by-step actions (not a checklist; a sequence)
- What "viable" means (prerequisites + success criteria)
- Current challenges (backlogs, recent policy changes)

**What to avoid:**
- Implying this is the "recommended" option
- Glossing over timelines ("3 weeks")
- Omitting that approval is not guaranteed
- Using outdated processing times

**Template trigger phrase:**
> "**Fastest pathway:** [NAME]. **Timeline:** [SEQUENCE] → [OUTCOME] ([TOTAL MONTHS]). **Current Status:** [BACKLOG/STANDARD]. Step 1: [ACTION]—[TIMELINE]. Step 2: [ACTION]—[TIMELINE]. What 'viable' means: [PREREQUISITES]. **Current challenge:** [BACKLOG/POLICY FLAG]."

---

### **D: A Fallback If The Primary Path Fails**
**What to include:**
- One alternative pathway (not all alternatives)
- What would trigger the fallback (specific condition)
- How it differs from primary path (requirements, timeline, barriers)
- When to consider it (explicit scenarios)

**What to avoid:**
- Suggesting fallback is "easier" or "better"
- Multiple fallbacks (keep it to one)
- Vague trigger conditions

**Template trigger phrase:**
> "**If [PRIMARY PATH] is not viable:** **Alternative:** [FALLBACK NAME]. **Triggers:** [SPECIFIC CONDITION]. Step 1: [ACTION]—[TIMELINE]. What changes: [REQUIREMENTS]. **Additional barrier:** [NEW DIFFICULTY]. When to consider: [EXPLICIT SCENARIOS]."

## Tone Rules (Non-Negotiable)

### **RULE 1: Specificity Over Vagueness**
| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do |
|---------|-------|
| "Good LGBTQ+ protections" | "Hate crime law (Law 5/99) covers sexual orientation; conviction carries 1–5 years imprisonment" |
| "Good income requirements" | "€920/month from passive income source (pensions, investments, rental income)" |
| "Takes a while" | "Current backlog: 10–14 months (up from standard 6–7 months as of January 2026)" |
| "Hard to get documentation" | "Requires 1+ year of tax returns; if you have recent employment or irregular income, obtaining IRS documentation takes 2–4 weeks" |

### **RULE 2: Name Barriers; Don't Soften Them**
| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do |
|---------|-------|
| "Just need to prove income" | "Must prove €920/month passive income with 1+ year documentation; difficulty if you lack tax history; solution: D6 pathway accepts liquid savings instead" |
| "Routine interview" | "Credibility assessment is the primary reason for claim denial in refugee cases; if documentation is weak, approval risk is high" |
| "Straightforward process" | "Three separate procedural stages (IRCC eligibility, biometrics, IRB hearing); missing one deadline results in automatic dismissal" |
| "Pretty affordable" | "Visa fees $200–$1,500; lawyer fees typically $3,000–$15,000; living expenses during 10–14 month backlog" |

### **RULE 3: Distinguish Law From Enforcement**
| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do |
|---------|-------|
| "Portugal will protect you" | "Portugal has hate crime law + abortion access. Legal protection ≠ practical safety. Research on-the-ground conditions through local LGBTQ+ and reproductive rights organizations" |
| "Canada is a safe country" | "Canada has federal refugee protections + comprehensive hate crime law. However, refugee claims are determined individually; approval is not guaranteed" |
| "Germany respects your rights" | "Germany has constitutional gender identity protections. Germany also has rising far-right political activity; research local conditions in your destination city" |

### **RULE 4: No False Certainty**
| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do |
|---------|-------|
| "You will be approved" | "Approval depends on documentation quality, credibility assessment, and policy at time of decision; approval is not guaranteed" |
| "This will take 3 weeks" | "Standard processing is 3 weeks; current backlog extends this to 8 weeks; your individual timeline may vary" |
| "You're definitely safe if you reach Germany" | "Germany offers legal protection. Your actual safety depends on your specific circumstances and on-the-ground enforcement" |
| "You can do this" | "This pathway is available if you meet these preconditions. Viability depends on your situation and a lawyer's assessment" |

### **RULE 5: Respect Fear; Don't Amplify It**
| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do |
|---------|-------|
| "Don't worry, it will all work out" (minimizes fear) | "This decision carries real risk. A lawyer can help you assess whether the risk is justified in your case" |
| "You'll definitely fail if [X]" (catastrophizes) | "If [X], approval risk increases significantly. A lawyer can assess your specific situation" |
| "It's impossible" (hopelessness) | "This pathway is difficult; here's why. If this path doesn't work, here's an alternative" |
| "It's so easy" (false hope) | "This pathway requires [STEPS] over [TIMELINE]. Feasibility depends on whether you can meet these requirements" |

## Intake Question Language (Reference for Writing Section B)

**Link barriers directly to intake answers. When user answered "No" to these, what becomes difficult?**

1. **Physical Presence** ("Can you physically reach the country?")
   - If No → All Protection/Asylum paths eliminated
   - Use: "Physical presence required; if you cannot travel, this is unavailable"

2. **Heritage Documentation** ("Do you have qualifying ancestry?")
   - If No → All ancestry/citizenship-by-descent paths eliminated
   - Use: "Requires documented [ANCESTOR]; difficult if you lack genealogical records"

3. **Refugee Ground** ("Is your threat tied to a recognized persecution ground?")
   - If No → All refugee/asylum paths eliminated
   - Use: "Must demonstrate persecution based on [GROUND]; difficulty if threat is economic or non-persecution-based"

4. **Documentation** ("Can you produce credible evidence of your threat?")
   - If No → All paths requiring evidentiary support weakened; approval risk high
   - Use: "Requires documentation of [EVIDENCE]; credibility assessment is primary reason for denial"

5. **Follow-Up Compliance** ("Can you attend mandatory procedural appointments?")
   - If No → All paths with multi-stage procedures eliminated
   - Use: "Requires attendance at [APPOINTMENTS]; missing one deadline results in dismissal"

6. **Reproductive Freedom** ("Is your threat linked to reproductive persecution?")
   - If Yes → Must eliminate destinations flagged "Highly Restricted"
   - Use: "Eliminates destinations with poor reproductive access because [REASON]"

7. **Legal Support** ("Do you have legal representation or advocacy access?")
   - If No → All paths with credibility/legal determination weakened
   - Use: "Requires legal representation for [STAGE]; pro-bono resources available but backlogged"

## Data Currency & Verification

**Every result must include:**
- [ ] Date of research: "Current as of January 2026"
- [ ] Source citations: Government ministry OR recognized NGO (ILGA, UNHCR, HRW, Center for Reproductive Rights)
- [ ] Confidence level: "High confidence," "Moderate confidence," or "Conditional—[flag pending change]"
- [ ] For timelines: "Standard processing [X] months; current backlog [Y] months (as of January 2026)"

**Before publishing, verify:**
- [ ] Data reflects Passagr research file (passagr_research.md)
- [ ] No post-January 2026 changes assumed
- [ ] If policy flagged as "pending" or "conditional" in research, state that explicitly
- [ ] Processing times include current backlogs (IRCC, Australia, Spain, etc.)

## The Mandatory Disclaimer

**Every result card must be accompanied by:**
- The full "What This Tool Cannot Do" disclaimer (from SAFETY_FIRST_OUTPUT_FRAMING.md, Part 3)
- OR a shortened version linking to the full disclaimer
- Users must understand: This is not legal advice; they need a lawyer; outcomes are not guaranteed; laws change

**Non-negotiable text:**
> "Passagr is not a lawyer. This information does not constitute legal advice. You must consult a qualified immigration attorney licensed in the destination country before making any decision."

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### **Pitfall 1: Comparative Language**
❌ "Spain is faster than Canada"
✅ "Spain's Digital Nomad Visa: 20 business days. Canada Express Entry: 10–14 months (backlog). Choose based on your specific requirements, not speed alone."

### **Pitfall 2: Motivational Tone**
❌ "You've got this!"
✅ "This pathway is viable if you meet these preconditions. Viability depends on your specific situation; consult a lawyer."

### **Pitfall 3: Oversimplification**
❌ "Portugal requires €920/month"
✅ "Portugal D7 requires €920/month from a passive income source (pensions, investments, rental income, dividends—not employment). Documented with 1+ years of tax returns or account statements."

### **Pitfall 4: Conflating Law with Safety**
❌ "Canada is safe for LGBTQ+ people" (implies personal safety)
✅ "Canada has federal hate crime legislation including sexual orientation and gender identity. Actual safety depends on your location, perpetrators, local enforcement, and on-the-ground conditions; research with local LGBTQ+ organizations."

### **Pitfall 5: Outdated Processing Times**
❌ "Canada Express Entry takes 6 months" (2024 standard; not current)
✅ "Canada Express Entry: Standard processing 6–7 months; current backlog 10–14 months (as of January 2026)."

### **Pitfall 6: Advice Tone**
❌ "You should apply for the Spain visa"
✅ "Spain's Digital Nomad Visa is an option if you meet these requirements. Decide based on your situation and a lawyer's assessment."

## For Content Review: Editing Checklist

Before approving any result card, an editor should verify:

### **Accuracy Check**
- [ ] All figures match Passagr research (passagr_research.md)
- [ ] Processing times cite January 2026 data + current backlogs
- [ ] Legal protections are correctly stated (check source in research)
- [ ] Intake question references match the actual questions (Agent 2)

### **Tone Check**
- [ ] No words: "just," "simply," "easy," "guaranteed," "you can do this," "don't worry," "you'll be okay"
- [ ] No superlatives: "best," "worst," "safest," "fastest" (unless reporting comparative fact)
- [ ] All soft claims converted to specific claims
- [ ] All barriers named plainly (no softening or minimization)

### **Completeness Check**
- [ ] All four sections present (A, B, C, D)
- [ ] Fallback pathway uses different preconditions (not same path slower)
- [ ] Data source + date included
- [ ] Disclaimer attached or linked
- [ ] Next steps clear (lawyer, ministry verification, NGO research)

### **Ethics Check**
- [ ] No legal advice given (only law described)
- [ ] No false certainty about outcomes
- [ ] No false hope or false doom
- [ ] Fear respected, not amplified
- [ ] User agency centered (information supports their choice)

## Questions to Ask When Drafting

1. **"What does this actually mean?"** (Replace vague language with specific details)
2. **"Is this from the research or speculation?"** (Only cite Passagr data; flag anything inferred)
3. **"What happens if the user is rejected?"** (Acknowledge this possibility; don't guarantee approval)
4. **"How does this connect to the intake questions?"** (If it doesn't, is it relevant?)
5. **"Would a user in a trauma state find this reassuring or accurate?"** (Choose accuracy)
6. **"Have laws changed since January 2026?"** (If uncertain, direct user to verify with official ministry)
7. **"Could this create false hope?"** (If yes, rewrite to state realistic risks)
